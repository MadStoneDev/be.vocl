It started as politeness. It became a personality.

The first time, I was new in the flat and grateful for a warm plate. "This is incredible," I said, and meant it the way you mean anything at 9pm on a Sunday when someone has cooked for you. She lit up. She told me it was her nan's recipe. She made it again the next week.

And the next. And the next.

I am now the official taste-tester of a lasagne I would not feed to an enemy. There is a layer of something I have never identified. I have learned to chew thoughtfully and nod, like a man on television tasting wine.

I will never tell her. Some lies are load-bearing.

What's the kind little lie you've kept going far too long?
