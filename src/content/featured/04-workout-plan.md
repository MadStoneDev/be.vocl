Here's the whole secret: the first week, I wasn't allowed to run.

I'd tried the apps before and quit by day four, wheezing and resentful. So this time I made the bar humiliatingly low. Week one: put shoes on, walk to the end of the street, come home. That's it. If I did more, fine, but the *job* was the shoes.

Turns out the shoes were the hard part. Once they were on I usually kept going.

Week three I jogged between two lamp posts and felt like Mo Farah. Week eight I ran 5k without stopping and cried a little, which I'm choosing to call hydration.

The plan that works is the one you're slightly embarrassed is too easy.
