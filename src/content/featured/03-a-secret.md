Every house has one forbidden shelf. Ours was the dented biscuit tin above the stove, the one Gran would glance at sometimes mid-sentence and then look away from.

We invented theories. Money. Letters. A gun, said my cousin, who watched too many films.

The week after the funeral we finally took it down. Inside: every birthday card any of us had ever made her. The terrible ones from when we were four, all glitter and backwards letters. Cinema tickets. A pressed flower from a wedding. A child's tooth in a twist of tissue.

She hadn't been hiding a secret. She'd been keeping us.

What did you find out about someone only after they were gone?
