The car park was empty in that specific way that makes you check your phone for the time twice.

I almost turned back. The torch beam only reached a few metres and the forest does that thing where it breathes. But I'd told myself I'd do this, and there's a version of me I'm trying to become who keeps small promises like that.

By the time I reached the saddle the sky had gone the colour of a peach you're not sure is ripe. Then the sun came over the far ridge and the whole valley lit up at once, frost smoking off the grass, and I just stood there holding a thermos like an idiot, grinning.

Nobody told me about this trail. I'm telling you.

Where's the place you go when you need to remember you're alive?
