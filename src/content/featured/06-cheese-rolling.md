There is a hill in Gloucestershire so steep that standing on it feels like a dare. Once a year, sensible people gather at the top, a man rolls an actual wheel of cheese down it, and then everyone throws themselves after it.

I had three pints of courage and one of regret.

The first two seconds I was running. After that I was no longer in charge of my own legs. The hill took over, physics took over, and I became a rumour of a man tumbling through grass. I overtook the cheese. I overtook a paramedic's expectations.

I came 14th. I lost a shoe and gained a story. My knee still clicks when it rains.

Would I do it again? Absolutely not. Will I talk about it at every dinner for the rest of my life? Obviously.
